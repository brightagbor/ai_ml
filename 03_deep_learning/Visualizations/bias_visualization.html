Understanding Bias in Neural Networks

The bias parameter (b) in a neural network acts as a horizontal shift for the activation function. It controls the threshold at which the neuron activates.

üîµ Positive Bias (b > 0)

Shifts the curve LEFT, making the neuron activate more easily. The neuron reaches 50% activation at x = -b/w instead of x = 0.

üî¥ Negative Bias (b < 0)

Shifts the curve RIGHT, making the neuron activate later. The neuron needs larger positive inputs to reach the same activation level.
